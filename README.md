# 2024-2025-N-N-Ecommerce-Sales-Trend-Analysis
N&amp;N Electronics sought to shift focus from rapid expansion toward profitability optimization. This analysis uncovered key revenue drivers, exposed a persistent 8.6% return leakage, and identified high-value customer segments to guide smarter budget and portfolio decisions.

## Key Analysis Areas & Project Objectives
Insights and recommendations are provided on the following key areas:
1. **Revenue Trends and Profit Drivers**
    - **Stakeholder:** Finance Team
    - **Objectives:** Understand revenue and profit drivers and identify high-performing product categories to drive Portfolio Rationalization, allowing the team to prioritize high-margin assets and phase out low-contribution products.
    - **Metrics:** Gross Sales, Net Sales, Final Realized Sales, Gross Profit, Profit Margin %
    - **Dimensions:** Order Date, Product Category, Product Name
2. **Marketing Channel Evaluation**
    - **Stakeholder:** Marketing Team
    - **Objectives:** Evaluate channel efficiency to optimize budget allocation, shifting focus from high-volume acquisition to high-value customer retention.
    - **Metrics:** Net Sales, Acquisition Volume, High-Value Customer Count (based on Net Sales)
    - **Dimensions:** Order Date, Marketing Channel
3. **Regional Performance**
    - **Stakeholders:** Marketing Team
    - **Objectives:** Surface regional trends to move away from "one-size-fits-all" campaigns toward localized marketing strategies tailored to specific geographic demands.
    - **Metrics:** Net Sales
    - **Dimensions:** Order Date, Product Category, Region, Marketing Channel
4. **Product Quality & Return Analysis**
    - **Stakeholders:** Product Team
    - **Objectives:** Identify quality-related friction that affect customer experience by analyzing category-specific return rates. This enables the Product Team to further investigate root causes.
    - **Metrics:** Return Rate, Orders, Revenue Leakage ($)
    - **Dimensions:** Product Category, Products

  
## Executive Summary
Based on the 2024–2025 performance analysis across revenue trends, marketing channels, regional performance, and product returns, the following key findings and recommendations were identified.

**Key Findings:**
- Revenue grew 16% YoY with two predictable seasonal peaks driving the majority of annual sales. However, Q4 growth is volume-led — AOV declines 8–16% as customers shift toward lower-ticket items and concentrated promotional activity, compressing margins during the highest-revenue period.
- Smartphones and Headphones are the two Core Driver categories, contributing ~$36M combined. Laptops and Tablets underperform on both margin and revenue, flagging them as candidates for portfolio rationalization. Smartwatches represent an underleveraged asset with a 33% margin, the lowest return rate in the portfolio (~7.8%), yet having low contribution to revenue at ~$5M.
- Paid Search dominates acquisition at ~4,000 customers but converts only 9% into High-Value customers. Email, by contrast, converts 65% of its 1,000 acquisitions — revealing a clear quality and quantity comparision across channels.
- Direct channel delivers strong customer quality at 54% High-Value conversion rate, representing high-value revenue that requires no paid acquisition spend.
- APAC contributes 51% of global revenue, anchored by China and India. LATAM shows zero Smartphone sales across the full 24-month period.
- A consistent 8.6% return-driven revenue leakage persists across all categories with no single outlier category, indicating systemic friction that erodes Final Realized Sales company-wide.

**Key Recommendations:**
- Initiate portfolio rationalization by phasing out the lowest-contributing Laptop and Tablet SKUs, redirecting investment toward Smartphones, Headphones, and Smartwatches.
- Reallocate a portion of Paid Search budget toward Email retention campaigns to improve High-Value Customer conversion and long-term marketing efficiency.
- Introduce a Q4 bundle strategy pairing high-ticket items with lower-ticket accessories to defend AOV during the Year-End volume surge.
- Develop region-specific campaign strategies aligned with local demand patterns, prioritizing Smartphone growth in LATAM after investigation of its absence.
- Launch a cross-functional investigation into systemic return friction, with priority return reduction targeting Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra and iPhone 15 Pro — the two highest leakage products at $620K and $597K respectively.
